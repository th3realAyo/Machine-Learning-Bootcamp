THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEEN AI, ML, DL, GenAI.

Hirarchy = AI => ML => DL => GenAI.
NOTE: 

Artificial Intelligence (AI):

1: AI is the broad concept of making machines think and act like humans. It involves creating systems that can learn, reason, and solve problems like humans. Examples include chess bots, self-driving cars, and virtual assistants such as Siri and Alexa.

AI itself does not always learn from data. Some AI systems simply follow rule-based instructions.

AI is the broader field, while other technologies (such as Machine Learning and Deep Learning) are subsets of it.

2: MACHINE LEARNING: Subset of AI, it's how we makes machines/systems learn/solve task automatically from data/patterns instead of explicitly hardcoding every rule. i.e Spam detection, Credit Card Fraud Detection , Recommendation System.

The machine learn/teaches itself from the data provided.

3: DEEP LEARNING: Subset of ML that uses artificial neural network to learn from large amount of data - inspired by the how the human brain works. i.e Image detection, Voice assistance, Autonomous driving vision systems. 

DL needs a lot of data, computing power & GPU to train it.

4: GEN AI: Subset of DL that focuses of creating new content (audio, video, code) by learning from existing data/examples. i.e ChatGPT (Text Generation), Dall E (Image Generation), GitHub Copilot (Coding Assistant)
